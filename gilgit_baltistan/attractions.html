Nanga Parbat

Nanga Parbat, known as the "Killer Mountain," is the ninth-highest mountain in the world, standing at 8,126 meters above sea level. It is located in the Diamer District of Gilgit-Baltistan and is a favorite among trekkers and mountaineers due to its challenging climb and breathtaking views. The mountain is also a popular spot for tourists who want to experience the raw beauty of the Himalayas.

Hunza Valley

Hunza Valley is one of the most beautiful and serene valleys in Pakistan, located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region. Surrounded by snow-capped peaks, including Rakaposhi and Ultar Sar, Hunza Valley is a paradise for nature lovers and photographers. The valley is also known for its rich cultural heritage, hospitable people, and ancient forts like Baltit and Altit.

Deosai National Park

Deosai National Park, often referred to as the "Land of Giants," is one of the highest plateaus in the world, with an average elevation of 4,114 meters above sea level. The park is famous for its stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and vast meadows that bloom with colorful flowers during the summer. It is also home to the Himalayan brown bear, making it a key destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

Skardu

Skardu is a picturesque town nestled in the heart of Gilgit-Baltistan, surrounded by the towering peaks of the Karakoram Range. It serves as the gateway to some of the world's highest mountains, including K2. Skardu is known for its stunning landscapes, tranquil lakes like Shangrila and Satpara, and the historic Skardu Fort. The town is a base for trekkers and adventurers exploring the Baltoro Glacier and other iconic sites.
